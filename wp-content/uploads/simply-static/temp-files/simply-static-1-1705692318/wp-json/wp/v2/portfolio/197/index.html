Define the Workshop Objective: Determine the specific focus of your workshop. Are you teaching a particular printing technique, such as screen printing or block printing? Or are you covering a broader range of printing methods? Clarify your objective to guide the workshop preparation.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Plan the Workshop Duration: Decide on the duration of your workshop. Consider the complexity of the techniques you’ll be teaching and the time required for participants to practice and create their prints. It could be a one-day workshop or spread over multiple sessions.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Secure a Venue: Find a suitable venue for your workshop. Look for a space that has enough room for participants to work comfortably and for you to set up the necessary equipment and supplies. Ensure the venue has access to utilities like water and electricity, depending on the printing techniques you’ll be teaching.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

Gather Equipment and Supplies: Determine the specific printing techniques you’ll be covering and gather the necessary equipment and supplies accordingly. This might include printing screens, squeegees, ink, printing blocks, carving tools, printing presses (if applicable), paper, fabric, and any other materials needed for the chosen techniques.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
